Digital literacy 
is the ability to effectively use digital technologies to access, evaluate, create, and communicate information. For students, useful digital tools include learning management systems, online research databases, productivity apps, and collaboration platforms that enhance study and teamwork. Safe internet practices—such as using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and respecting privacy—help protect personal information. Building a professional online presence through platforms like LinkedIn allows students to showcase skills and achievements responsibly.
 Email etiquette,
 including clear subject lines, polite language, concise messages, and proper sign-offs, ensures effective and respectful communication in academic and professional settings.
