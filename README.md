📌 TASK 5: Phishing Awareness

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity.

A typical phishing attack involves:

Receiving a fake message from a trusted source
Clicking a malicious link
Being redirected to a fake website
Entering sensitive information, which is then stolen

Students are often targeted due to lack of verification habits. Consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

✅ Prevention Checklist
Verify sender email before clicking links
Do not share OTPs or passwords
Use strong and unique passwords
Enable two-factor authentication
Avoid suspicious links
Check website URLs carefully
Avoid public Wi-Fi for transactions
Keep apps and software updated
Verify UPI receiver before sending money
Never approve unknown payment requests
Report fraud at cybercrime.gov.in
Call 1930 in case of fraud
📌 Reflection

One habit I will adopt is verifying every link and email before taking action, even if it appears trustworthy. This task increased my awareness of cyber threats and highlighted the importance of spreading awareness among others.
